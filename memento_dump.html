Yoru guide ↗
夜 · wind down · fall asleep
mindfulnesssleepbreathingreactvite

Yoru (夜, 'night') is a wind-down for the end of the day and nothing else — one dim, frameless screen, typeset in a single Japanese hand. Everything lives behind the glyph 夜: tap it to pick a soundscape and length, tune the sound, choose how the screen behaves, and breathe along if you like. A slowing breath and a synthesised, blendable soundscape carry you down, ebbing to true silence by the end so nothing tethers you awake. Set down whatever you're carrying into tomorrow; it's held for the night and then let go.

What's inside
  • A single dim, frameless night in a Japanese mincho hand, with three moods — cool Night, lilac Moonlight, warm Candlelight
  • Everything's behind the glyph: tap 夜 for Settings — soundscape, session length (15–90'), a full audio tuner, display mode, breathwork
  • A layer-blend audio mixer (like the best ambient apps): blend Rain, Waves, Wind, Leaves, a warm noise floor and a low drone, then shape Volume, Brightness, Motion, Pace and Stereo width — all synthesised, animal- and city-free, auditioned live
  • Optional breathwork: a breathing orb that slows as the night goes (a lengthening exhale, or 4·7·8) — or leave it off for sound alone
  • The sound breathes with you and ebbs to true silence by the finish — it always flows out, never cuts
  • Three display modes: stay lit, go dark to a night sky, or truly turn the screen off to save power (and wake back to lit)
  • The 'go dark' sky is alive: drifting stars, Mount Fuji, the moon at tonight's real phase and place, and season-aware drift — sakura, fireflies, momiji or snow
  • Set down a single thought for tomorrow — write it, let it out of sight, and it's discarded when the night ends; greets you by name at the close
  • Resumes within the same night, starts fresh on a new one — all on this device, no account
