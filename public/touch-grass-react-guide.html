// feature guide

Everything Touch Grass does.

Touch Grass is a small ritual disguised as an app. Step away from the screen and go outside; walk for as long as you like; come back, and a single strange find is waiting for you. The whole thing lives inside one illustrated tarot card — a card, a button, a discovery at a time.

The card is tied to the world around you. With your permission it reads your location, so the daylight, the weather, the moon's phase and the rare moments of the year are all true to where and when you actually are — the painted sky mirrors the sky outside your window, and every find is tinted by the hour, the season and the conditions you walk through. This page lays all of it out, feature by feature.

// how to use

  1. Open the app. A fresh tagline greets you — a quiet invitation, different every time.
  2. Tap Head outside and go. The timer saves immediately — close the tab, lock your screen, it doesn't matter.
  3. Walk for however long you like. Longer walks shift the odds toward rarer finds.
  4. Come back, open the app, tap I'm back.
  5. See what you found. Tap Step inside when you're ready to go again — a fresh tagline will be waiting.
tagline

Home screen tagline

Every time you arrive at the home screen — on first open, after stepping inside from a result, or after closing settings — a fresh tagline is generated by Claude. Dreamy, witty, a quiet play on words about leaving your screen behind. A thin animated line sweeps across while it loads, replaced by the text when ready. No two visits look quite the same.

If no API key is set, a tagline is picked at random from a built-in list instead — no loading, no delay.

timer

Departure timer

Tap Head outside and the timer starts immediately — your departure timestamp is saved to localStorage the moment you tap. Close the tab, lock your screen, restart the browser entirely. When you come back, the app picks up exactly where you left off and uses the elapsed duration to determine what you found.

away

While you're out

A minimal screen shows your time out and departure time on one line: Out for 4m 32s · since 5:23 PM. The elapsed time is color-coded as the walk grows:

  • Under 30 min — plain
  • 30 min – 1 hour — uncommon teal
  • 1 hour – 4 hours — rare amber
  • 4 hours and beyond — legendary purple

Close the tab at any point — when you return and tap I'm back, the elapsed time is calculated from your saved departure timestamp regardless.

finds

The finds

Every walk returns exactly one find — always. Never more, never less. Walk duration affects its rarity, not the quantity. Each is conjured by Claude like an entry in a grimoire or the face of an unknown tarot card: surreal, esoteric, faintly macabre, with a thread of cosmic dread. Hushed and strange, kept brief — no more than twenty-two words — and coloured by the hour, season, weather, moon, the lie of the land around you, and the rare moments of the year you walked through. Its subject ranges widely from walk to walk — stone, water, a sound, a forgotten object, the occasional rare insect — never settling on one motif. Four tiers of escalating strangeness:

  • common A small wrongness, a quiet omen — the world tilting half a degree.
  • uncommon An esoteric object or sign, clearly impossible, humming with hidden meaning.
  • rare A divinatory apparition — the veil thinning, something looking back.
  • legendary A cosmic, mythic revelation: vast, ancient, indifferent — the kind that rearranges you.

If no API key is set, finds come from a built-in pool — the "old book" — instead. If a key is set but the call fails, a find from the old book is used and a quiet ⚠ the oracle was silent note appears — bottom-right of the result screen.

rarity

Odds by walk length

The longer you walk, the better your odds of finding something rare. Quantity never changes — just what you find.

Walk length common uncommon rare legendary
Under 30 min 60% 30% 8% 2%
30 – 59 min 48% 36% 13% 3%
60 min + 30% 42% 20% 8%
divining

Divining your find

When you tap I'm back, your rarity tier is rolled immediately — before the API call is made. The screen shows the tier name while the find is conjured, so you know what's coming before it arrives. Uncommon, rare, and legendary are color-coded. Common just says Divining your find… — no announcement for the everyday. A sweep line runs beneath while the call is in flight.

loop

The loop

The app moves in a deliberate circle. Tap Step inside on the result screen and you return to the home screen — a fresh tagline loads, a new invitation. Tap Head outside when you're ready and the next loop begins. Nothing resets until you choose to start again.

scene

A living tarot card

The whole app lives inside an illustrated tarot card — a parchment frame edged with a fine double keyline and a small black glyph at each corner (a crescent, a star, a sun, an eye), a full-bleed scene behind the words, and a title beneath each panel: The Threshold, The Wandering, The Omen, The Discovery, The Keeper. The art runs edge to edge; the text floats over a soft gradient scrim so it always stays readable. The surface the card rests on is gradient too — inverted against the card's interior, and the opposite temperature, so a dark cool night scene sits on a warm pale surround and a bright day on a deep one.

sky

Sky, sun & moon

The sky is painted from the device clock and calendar — four moods (dawn, day, dusk, night) whose thresholds shift with the season, so a midsummer evening stays bright hours longer than a winter one. A faced sun presides over the day, a faced moon over the night — each with a calm expression, teal eyes, and rosy cheeks. The horizon never goes fully black: a warm glow and scattered city lights linger along it after dark.

stars

Your constellation overhead

After dusk, the constellation of the current zodiac sign rises in the corner — connect-the-dot stars following the real shape of each sign, glowing like small embers. Around it a faint field of background stars drifts and twinkles, reseeded every calendar day, so the night sky is never quite the same twice. You can turn the signs off in the Keeper if you prefer a plain sky.

grass

Grass, flowers & small life

A band of grass, wildflowers, the odd bush and weed grows along the bottom, recolored each season — fresh greens in spring, gold in summer, russet in autumn, muted in winter (no blooms then). The whole patch is regrown each calendar day, so its shapes and placement shift daily and never sit the same way twice. By day a few butterflies wander across; after dark, fireflies drift and pulse over the grass. And the season drifts through the air: tumbling leaves in autumn, blossom petals in spring.

sound

A living soundscape

An ambient bed plays across every screen, synthesized live rather than looped — there are no audio files at all. It's made for headphones and a long walk: every sound is placed across a stereo field inside a soft, warm room that grows from an intimate city street to vast mountain air, and the whole soundscape quietly breathes — swelling with life, then easing toward near-stillness, the way real outdoors does. Wind moves in gusts that rise and fall with the true wind; when rain stops you'll hear it drip away and the birds return. Dawn brings a fuller chorus, dusk hands the air to crickets, deep night keeps an owl or a far-off frog. Stay a while and rare, distant things drift past — a church bell on the hour, a spring cuckoo, a woodpecker, a wolf on a clear winter night, a foghorn in the mist, a shimmer when a star falls. And deep beneath it all: the roll of thunder, a city's low rumble. Nothing is loud; nothing repeats.

feedback

Taps & the reveal

Buttons answer with a soft tap. Stepping outside plays a gentle descending breath; returning to a discovery is met with a warm ascending shimmer — a small aha, distinct from every other sound. On phones, each is paired with a light haptic tick. The first tap on the page is what wakes the audio (a browser requirement).

motion

Respects reduced motion

If your device is set to reduce motion — or you turn Motion off in the Keeper — the drifting stars, fireflies, butterflies, falling snow, drifting clouds, rain and shooting stars all freeze in place. The scene stays, the movement stops.

place

Your real sky

With your permission, the app reads your location once (and only your rough coordinates, kept in your browser). From it, daylight becomes exact — the real sunrise, sunset and twilight where you are, so a midsummer dusk lingers as long as it truly does. Deny it, or turn Place off in the Keeper, and everything falls back gracefully to the seasonal estimates. Your rough coordinates are only ever sent to the open services that answer back — weather, elevation, and a coarse reverse-geocode — never stored anywhere but your own browser.

biome

A sense of where you are

From your rough location the app reads the lie of the land — coast, forest, city, mountain or open plain — and lets it colour both the finds and the sound. The oracle leans toward salt and tideline by the sea, stone and thin air on a mountain, brick and the hum of streets in a city. The soundscape follows: surf and gulls at the coast, a leaf-rustle and a woodpecker in forest, a low rumble and pigeons in the city, a thin keening wind and a raptor's cry on the heights. Kept deliberately coarse — a single word, never an address.

light

Golden hour & blue hour

The card mirrors not only the weather but the quality of the light. As the sun nears the horizon — at dawn and again at dusk — the sky warms through amber and rose, the sun deepens toward a sunset orange, and the grass catches the glow. In the brief blue hour after sundown the sky sinks to deep indigo with a thin band of warmth left along the horizon. It eases in and out with the sun's true angle, never a hard switch, and softens under cloud. (Location on; otherwise the plain seasonal sky.)

weather

The weather outside, on the card

The scene mirrors the real sky. Clouds gather to the true cloud cover (a few drifting puffs, or a full grey overcast that veils the sun and hides the stars). Rain falls when it's raining, snow when it's snowing — never by guesswork. Fog rolls in as a soft, desaturating bank; thunderstorms darken the sky and flash. Wind speeds the clouds and stirs the air. The soundscape follows too — rain patter, distant thunder, a wind that rises with the real gusts.

moon

The true moon

The moon shows tonight's real phase. The faced disc stays whole and central — never a thin sliver — with a soft shadow drawn along the terminator across the unlit side. At full moon it's the bare, bright face; new and crescent nights wear more of the shadow, the face still watching through it.

moments

Rare moments through the year

On certain days the sky and the finds turn special — the solstices and equinoxes, the old cross-quarter festivals, the great meteor showers, the night of a full or new moon, the Sun's passage into each zodiac sign, and more. On those days the find and the home-screen tagline are coloured by the moment (its name glows in its own colour on the home screen), and on meteor-shower nights real shooting stars streak the clear sky.

forecast

A glance at the day ahead

When location is on, the home screen offers a small reading of the sky above the title — the sky's augury. A little drawn glyph shows the weather (a sun, a cloud, fog, rain, snow, a storm's bolt), and a single line divines the day in plain words: 14° under a grey lid — climbing toward 19° before the dark draws it to 9°. The temperatures are tinted by their warmth — cool blues, mild greens, warm golds. Beneath, a soft note flags anything worth knowing before you set out — a breeze picks up later, a chance of rain — descriptors, not warnings, never red. Turn Place off and the augury simply isn't there.

countdown

The turning of the day

While you're out, a quiet line beneath the timer counts down to the next turn of the day — sunrise, noon, sunset or midnight, whichever comes next, from the real solar times where you are. A gentle sense of where the light is going — never something to watch.

reliquary

The Reliquary — your kept finds

Every find is kept. Tap the small teal lens tucked into the bottom-left grass (opposite the key) to open The Reliquary — a scrollable log of every walk, newest first, each a single compact line — its date, duration, rarity and the name of the find it brought back. The list rests below the sky and scrolls on its own as it fills, counting itself at the top. You can clear the most recent entry or the whole log, each behind a one-tap confirmation. It all lives in your browser, nowhere else.

setup

The Keeper

Tap the red key in the bottom-right corner to open The Keeper (its companion, the lens, sits opposite in the bottom-left). Enter your Anthropic API key — it stays in your browser and is only ever sent directly to Anthropic, when generating a find or tagline. Here you can also toggle Sound, the constellation Signs, Place (real location), Motion (freeze the scene), and the Daily call on or off — each shows its state, lit for on and dim for off. Without a key, everything still works — finds and taglines come from the old book instead.

daily call

A daily call to go out

Once a day, about two hours before sunset, the app can send a single soft notification in its own voice — a nudge to take today's walk while the light lasts, or, if you've already been out, a quiet acknowledgement and the find you brought home. Just one, never more. It's on by default (your browser asks permission the first time); switch it off any time with Daily call in the Keeper. Being a screen-free app with no server behind it, it reaches you best when you open the app around that hour — it won't chase you.

persistence

Survives everything

Walk state — departure time, result, status — is saved under one localStorage key and written automatically after every change. Your API key is stored separately under its own key, since it has a different lifecycle and shouldn't reset when a new walk starts. Close the tab mid-walk, mid-result, or mid-tagline — the app picks up correctly every time.

// the app is complete

That's all of it. Touch Grass is finished — every screen, every sound, every quiet detail above is in your hands now. There's no next version waiting in the wings; the most fitting thing left to do is close the tab and go outside.

// preview parameters

For the curious and the tinkerers: add any of these to the address bar to summon a condition without waiting for the world to provide it. Combine them freely — for instance ?tod=dusk&light=golden, or ?biome=coast&solo.

  • ?tod=dawn|day|dusk|night — force the time of day
  • ?season=spring|summer|autumn|winter — force the season
  • ?light=golden|blue|day|night — force the golden / blue hour
  • ?wx=clear|cloudy|overcast|fog|rain|snow|thunder — force the weather
  • ?moon=0–1 — force the moon's phase (0 new · 0.5 full · 1 new)
  • ?biome=coast|forest|city|mountain|plain — force the sense of place
  • ?surface=0–8 — preview a ground surface (the colour outside the card rotates daily)
  • ?event=<key> — force an almanac moment, e.g. ?event=perseids
  • ?solo — mute all but the biome's own sounds, to audition them
  • ?call=now — fire the daily-call notification straight away
