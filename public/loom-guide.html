Loom

weave your week, thread by thread

Loom is a five-minute weekly planner and a tribute to LucasArts' Loom (1990) and the SCUMM adventures. Every task is a thread: spin it, warp it across the days, gather it into a skein, and rank your threads by hand. It runs offline out of the box and can be backed by your own Notion database.

The idea

  • Threads & skeins. A thread is one task. A skein is a project or category you gather threads into — the Skeins view groups by skein.
  • The Week (the warp). The same threads, warped across the seven days. Add a thread straight into a day; fling it along the warp to another day; park anything undated on the distaff (a backlog rail) until you're ready.
  • The heatmap (no priority tags). Drag threads to rank them. Position alone sets the colour — the top thread burns ember-hot and each below cools through copper, gold and sage to slate. Past the top six, threads fall to a cold grey, so an overloaded day grows its own grey tail — no hard limit needed. (An Ivy-Lee idea: pick your few, in order.)
  • hottest → coolest → overflow
  • Tactile gestures. Swipe a thread right to weave it done, left to unravel (delete) it. Tap the knot to weave, tap the title to edit. Drag the grip to reorder (or focus it and use ↑/↓).

Frictionless entry

Everything is inline — no pop-ups. In Skeins, name a skein and spin a thread into it, or add straight to any skein's foot. In The Week, type into a day column. That's the whole five-minute ritual: lay the week out, drag the top few into order, and go.

Offline, or your own Notion

Loom keeps your week on the loom locally by default — a full demo on sample threads stored only on this device. To keep real threads and back them up, bind Loom to a Notion database. It takes a few minutes and needs no coding.

1 · Get a database

Easiest — duplicate the ready-made template. Open the Loom — Starter Template, an empty database with the exact columns already set up. Use the top-right ••• menu → Duplicate to make your own private copy in your workspace.

Open the Starter Template →

Or build your own — a Notion database with these five properties (names are exact):

  • Name — Title — the thread's text
  • Skein — Select — its project / category
  • Day — Date — the day it's warped to (leave empty for the backlog)
  • Order — Number — its manual rank (Loom writes this when you drag; drives the heat)
  • Done — Checkbox — woven

Any extra columns Notion adds by default are fine to keep or delete, as long as those five exist with these exact names.

2 · Create an integration & connect

An internal integration is just a key that lets this one app reach your database.

  1. Create it. Go to notion.so/my-integrations → New integration. Name it (e.g. Loom), pick the workspace your database lives in, leave the type Internal, and save.
  2. Copy the token. On the integration's page open Configuration (or Secrets), find the Internal Integration Secret, Show then Copy. It starts with ntn_ (older ones with secret_). Treat it like a password.
  3. Share the database with it. Open your Loom database in Notion → top-right ••• → Connections (or Add connections) → search your integration → select it. Without this the app sees nothing.
  4. Paste both into Loom. Open Loom → the ⚙ Settings (or the Guild verb) → paste the token and your database link, hit Test connection, then Weave live. Leave the database blank to use the app's built-in default.

Your token and database id are kept only in this browser and relayed to Notion through this site's proxy — never stored on a server.

Two moods

Loom ships with two SCUMM-flavoured palettes — a dark Twilight and a light Parchment. The ◐ button (top-right here and in the app) cycles them, and this guide follows your choice.
