The Daily Stoic
Field Guide

A handbook for implementing Marcus Aurelius's resilience, Seneca's self-examination, and Epictetus's freedom in modern daily life.

I. The Philosophical Foundation

Stoicism is not a set of abstract theories; it is a practical discipline—a operating system for the mind. This PWA acts as a private notebook and tracking ledger to build mental resilience, master dysfunctional reactions, and live in accordance with nature.

Epictetus's Axiom

The Dichotomy of Control

The root of all tranquility is separating what is Up to Me (our thoughts, desires, values, and actions) from what is Not Up to Me (the actions of others, external events, health, reputation, and the weather). When you concern yourself solely with your own choices, you become unshakeable.

Marcus Aurelius's Formula

Amor Fati (Love of Fate)

Marcus Aurelius wrote that the mind adapts and converts any obstacle to its path: "The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way." Amor Fati means not merely tolerating friction, but choosing to embrace it as raw material to practice patience, temperance, or courage.

Seneca's Evening Ledger

The Nightly Review

Seneca recommended reviewing each day before sleep, balancing the soul's accounts. By asking what ailments were cured, what vices resisted, and how one can improve, you replace self-judgment with constructive self-awareness.

II. The Daily Journey (Stepper)

The application coordinates your daily practices into a structured 4-step stepper at the core of the homepage.

  1. Step 1: Focus (Memento Mori)

    You are greeted by a visual grid representing the 52 weeks of the current calendar year. This keeps the reality of time close at hand. A Stoic asks: "If this week were my last, how would I spend it?"

  2. Step 2: Meditate (Daily Quote & Enchiridion)

    surfaces a quote tied directly to your cycle day. Tap the heart to add quotes to your custom Enchiridion—your personal emergency handbook of principles to consult in times of crisis.

  3. Step 3: Prepare (Premeditatio Malorum)

    Write down today's worries, anxieties, or obstacles. Once typed, actively sort them: categorizing each item as Up to Me (internals you can influence) or Not Up to Me (externals you must accept).

  4. Step 4: Reflect (Evening Review)

    Log your mood, highlight any dysfunctional passions encountered (Ego, Anxiety, Impatience, Anger), record Seneca's evening questions, and select which of the four cardinal virtues was most needed to close out your day.

Rule of Practice

The daily stepper detects unsaved changes across text inputs, worry lists, and passions. Always scroll to Step 4 and tap the primary Complete Reflection button to commit your day.

III. The Four Cardinal Virtues

Every day, the evening review prompts you to reflect on the four virtues. Ponder how they apply to the events you encountered:

  • Wisdom (Sophia): The ability to distinguish between what is good, what is bad, and what is indifferent. It guides our judgment in complex circumstances.
  • Courage (Andreia): Staying strong, acting justly, and standing firm in the face of physical danger, ethical challenges, or daily friction.
  • Justice (Dikaiosyne): Working for the common good. Stoics view humanity as members of a single body; acting selfishly or with anger violates our nature.
  • Temperance (Sophrosyne): Self-control, discipline, and moderation. Knowing when enough is enough, resisting excess, and staying anchored.

IV. Analytics & Dashboards

The app includes custom dashboards accessible from the top navigation dropdown on mobile, or the header links on desktop:

Dichotomy Dashboard

Spheres of Choice

Analyze your worries over time (30d, 90d, 365d, or All). Tracks your control ratio (percentage of concerns sorted as Up to Me) and your concern resolution rate. Custom word clouds visually display the concepts that occupy your mind.

Self-Knowledge Dashboard

Citadel of Temperance

Tracks recurring passions and judgments. It correlates your triggers to specific days of the week or times of day, identifying when your ego or impatience is most active, and recommends specific Stoic virtues and exercises as antidotes.

Fate Dashboard

Amor Fati Retrospective

Review past challenges. Revisit the obstacles you logged, review them in a rolling historical timeline (30, 90, 365 days ago), and rate how successfully you reframed those struggles.

V. Notion Cloud Synchronization

By default, all entries are stored locally on your device (offline-first). To sync data across devices, configure a secure Notion integration in Settings:

  1. Go to notion.so/my-integrations and create a new integration. Copy the Integration Token.
  2. Duplicate the official Starter Template Database.
  3. In the top right of your Notion database page, click the ... and select Connections, then select your new integration.
  4. Copy the database URL and paste it along with the token in the app's Settings panel.

VI. The 365-Day Cycle

The app operates on a 365-day cycle relative to your Cycle Start Date (set under Settings). On Day 365, upon saving your reflection, the app activates the Annual Retrospective & Celebration Screen.

This screen compiles your entire year's performance, summarizing your consistency, reframings, worries resolved, and passions conquered. You can download this summary card as a premium image. Once reviewed, closing the modal resets your cycle start date to today and archives your database entries, giving you a fresh ledger to begin the next year.
