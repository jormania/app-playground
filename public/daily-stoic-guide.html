The Daily Stoic
Field Guide

A handbook for implementing Marcus Aurelius's resilience, Seneca's self-examination, and Epictetus's freedom in modern daily life.

I. The Philosophical Foundation

Stoicism is not a set of abstract theories; it is a practical discipline — an operating system for the mind. This PWA acts as a private notebook and tracking ledger to build mental resilience, master dysfunctional reactions, and live in accordance with nature.

Epictetus's Axiom

The Dichotomy of Control

The root of all tranquility is separating what is Up to Me (our thoughts, desires, values, and actions) from what is Not Up to Me (the actions of others, external events, health, reputation, and the weather). When you concern yourself solely with your own choices, you become unshakeable.

Marcus Aurelius's Formula

Amor Fati (Love of Fate)

Marcus Aurelius wrote that the mind adapts and converts any obstacle to its path: "The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way." Amor Fati means not merely tolerating friction, but choosing to embrace it as raw material to practice patience, temperance, or courage.

Seneca's Evening Ledger

The Nightly Review

Seneca recommended reviewing each day before sleep, balancing the soul's accounts. By asking what ailments were cured, what vices resisted, and how one can improve, you replace self-judgment with constructive self-awareness.

II. The Daily Journey (Stepper)

The app coordinates your daily practice into a structured 4-step stepper at the core of the homepage. Each step is labelled with your position in the current cycle — "Day 4 of Week 2 of Cycle 5," for example — so you always know where you stand.

  1. Step 1: Focus (Memento Mori)

    A visual grid representing your entire lifespan in weeks (once your birth date is set in Settings) keeps the reality of time close at hand. A Stoic asks: "If this were my last week, how would I spend it?"

  2. Step 2: Meditate (Daily Quote & Enchiridion)

    Surfaces a quote tied to your absolute day count. Tap the heart to add it to your custom Enchiridion — your personal handbook of principles to consult in moments of crisis. Below the day's quote sits a second, slower one: the Quote of the Week, fixed for all seven days of the current week and themed to that week's Cardinal Virtue.

  3. Step 3: Prepare (Premeditatio Malorum & Spheres of Choice)

    Write down today's anticipated friction — obstacles, difficult people, or internal weaknesses you expect to meet. Then log today's worries and actively sort each one as Up to Me (internals you can influence) or Not Up to Me (externals you must accept).

  4. Step 4: Reflect (Evening Review)

    Log your mood, flag any dysfunctional passions you noticed in yourself (impatience, anxiety, concern for reputation, discontent, pride, craving), answer Seneca's three evening questions, name which Cardinal Virtue you leaned on, and record any Amor Fati reframe for the day's heaviest moment.

Rule of Practice

The daily stepper detects unsaved changes across text inputs, worry lists, and passions. Scroll to Step 4 and save your reflection to commit the day — every save writes to Notion (if configured) with a local fallback, so nothing is lost offline.

III. The Four Cardinal Virtues

Every week of a cycle is themed to one of the four Cardinal Virtues, always in the same order — Wisdom, Courage, Justice, then Temperance — before the cycle repeats. The Evening Review's virtue picker and the week's Quote of the Week both draw from this same rotation:

  • Wisdom (Sophia): The ability to distinguish between what is good, what is bad, and what is indifferent. It guides our judgment in complex circumstances.
  • Courage (Andreia): Staying strong, acting justly, and standing firm in the face of physical danger, ethical challenges, or daily friction.
  • Justice (Dikaiosyne): Working for the common good. Stoics view humanity as members of a single body; acting selfishly or with anger violates our nature.
  • Temperance (Sophrosyne): Self-control, discipline, and moderation. Knowing when enough is enough, resisting excess, and staying anchored.

IV. The 28-Day Virtue Cycle

Progress is organized around a repeating 28-day cycle — four 7-day weeks, each themed to one Cardinal Virtue in the fixed order above. Every cycle begins on a Monday, and week boundaries always land on a real Monday.

Rollover into a new cycle is automatic and non-destructive — your history is never archived or wiped when a cycle completes. Instead, the moment a cycle finishes, a dismissible retrospective screen appears once, summarizing that cycle's consistency rate, Amor Fati reframes, worries resolved, and passions tamed. Closing it simply acknowledges the milestone; the new cycle has already begun underneath it.

Every day, week, and cycle you've ever logged stays browsable afterward in the Digest (see the next section) — nothing about a completed cycle ever disappears.

V. Dashboards, Digest & Stats

Beyond the daily stepper, five screens are reachable from the top navigation (desktop header links, or the mobile dropdown). Three of them are interpretive dashboards — they mine your data for patterns and offer Stoic advice. The other two, Digest and Stats, are archival and numeric — they report, without judging.

Dashboard

Spheres of Choice

Your worry log over time: the ratio sorted Up to Me vs. Not Up to Me, your concern-resolution rate, and word clouds of what actually occupies your mind on each side of the dichotomy.

Dashboard

Passions & Judgments

Tracks your six recurring passions (impatience, anxiety, reputation, discontent, pride, craving) over time, surfaces your dominant one, correlates it to a day-of-week/time-of-day pattern, and recommends a specific Stoic exercise and quote as an antidote.

Dashboard

Amor Fati

Your reframe rate, your dominant obstacle category, a distribution chart across all five challenge types, and a retrospective timeline resurfacing obstacles you logged a while back — "you thought this was a big deal a month ago."

Archive

Digest

A single, newest-first list of every day you've ever logged, interspersed with a summary entry the moment a week or a cycle completes. Tap any day for a brief popup of that day's submission (quote, Premeditatio Malorum, Evening Interrogation answers, mood, Spheres of Choice entries, Amor Fati reframe, passions, virtue). A completed cycle's summary carries a "View full retrospective" link back to its complete stats.

Numbers, not judgment

Stats

Pure counts and rates — Stats never tells you what to do about a number; that framing belongs to the three dashboards above. It reports: current and longest streak, cycles completed, total favorited quotes, a period-scoped totals block (days journaled, dominant mood, Premeditatio Malorum completion rate, words written), a Virtue Week Breakdown table (consistency, average mood, and favorites per virtue-week across every cycle you've completed), a day-of-week logging chart, a mood distribution chart, and a 28-cell heatmap of the cycle currently in progress.

The shared period filter

Stats and all three dashboards share one filter, remembered across all four screens:

  • Cycle — the current cycle's own date range, from its start through today. A cycle only 5 days in reports on those 5 days, never padded backward into the previous cycle.
  • Quarter — the real calendar date 3 months before today (not a fixed count of cycles), so it always matches an actual quarter's length regardless of which months it spans.
  • Year — the real calendar date 1 year before today, correctly covering 366 days whenever the window crosses a leap day, rather than drifting a day short the way a fixed 365-day count eventually would.
  • All — your entire history, unfiltered.

VI. Notion Cloud Synchronization

By default, every entry is stored locally on your device (offline-first). To sync across devices and keep a permanent record, connect a Notion database in Settings — nothing is required to use the app without one.

Fastest path

Duplicate the Daily Stoic — Starter Template instead of building the database by hand — it already carries the exact schema below, empty and de-personalized.

  1. Go to notion.so/my-integrations and create a new integration. Copy the Integration Token.
  2. Duplicate the Starter Template into your own workspace — top-right ••• → Duplicate.
  3. Open the database, click ••• → Connections, and add your integration.
  4. In the app, open Settings, paste your Notion Integration Token and the Notion Database Link or ID, then tap Test & Save Connection — it checks every property and tells you if anything is missing.

Database Schema

The seven optional columns below (everything after Favorite) auto-upgrade onto your database on first connection if they're missing — you don't have to add them by hand even if you build the database yourself instead of duplicating the template.

Cycle Start Date

Set once in Settings — this is the Monday your first cycle began. It's a local, on-device setting, not a Notion property; everything about which cycle, week, and virtue you're in is computed from it. "Start Over From Cycle 1" in Settings resets it to the most recent Monday, for a clean restart.
