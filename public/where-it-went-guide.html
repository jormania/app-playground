Where It Went — guide

A clean, minimal personal finance web application that uses Notion as its backend database.

What it is

Where It Went is a specialized tracker for answering a simple question: "Where did my money go?" It leans into a clean, Github-inspired dark aesthetic, separating analytics and visualization from the actual data storage.

Everything is stored in your Notion, under your Notion integration token — the app holds no data of its own. It installs as a Progressive Web App (PWA) on desktop or mobile.

1. Quick Setup

To connect your own real, persistent collection, set it up directly in Notion, then point the app at it from Settings:

  1. Create an Integration: Go to Notion Integrations and create a new integration.
  2. Copy the Token: Copy the Internal Integration Secret.
  3. Prepare the Databases: Ensure you have a Transactions database, an Accounts database, and a Categories database in Notion. See §2.
  4. Share the databases: Open your Notion databases, click ••• → Connections, and add your integration.
  5. Get the Links: Copy the Notion link to each database.
  6. Connect it: Open Settings (⚙), paste the Integration Token and the database links (or IDs), and save.

2. The Notion Database Schema

Where It Went expects three interconnected databases. They must be named properly and their properties must match these definitions exactly.

Transactions Database

PropertyTypeNotes
NameTitleDescription of the transaction.
AmountNumberPositive for Income, negative for Expenses.
DateDateWhen the transaction occurred.
AccountRelationRelation to the Accounts DB (Limit: 1).
CategoryRelationRelation to the Categories DB (Limit: 1).

Categories Database

PropertyTypeNotes
NameTitleCategory name (e.g. Housing, Food, Salary).
TypeSelectMust be exactly Income or Expense.
DescriptionRich Text(Optional) Shows up as a description when selecting the category.
IconNotion IconThe app reads native Notion Emoji icons for categories. E.g. 🏠 Housing, 🍽️ Dining, 💡 Utilities, etc.

Accounts Database

PropertyTypeNotes
NameTitleAccount name (e.g. Checking, Credit Card).
TypeSelectAsset or Liability.
BalanceNumberCurrent balance (currently informational).

3. Core Views

Where It Went features two primary views within a single page application.

Dashboard

The top section contains Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) summarizing your financial health for the current month: Income, Expenses, and Net Cash Flow.

Below the KPIs, the Monthly Review intelligently analyzes your spending and presents actionable insights: month-over-month comparisons, missing recurring incomes, increased spending alerts in discretionary categories, and reflective questions to help you align your spending with your values.

It includes a sleek Chart.js bar graph that visualizes Monthly Spending vs Income over time, alongside a Recent Transactions list.

Transactions Ledger

The lower section is your complete ledger. You can sort by clicking the headers (Date, Description, Category, Account, Amount). The Add Transaction component dynamically filters available categories depending on whether you input a positive amount (Income) or a negative amount (Expense), and renders their emojis and descriptions inline.

4. Data Ownership & Sync

Where It Went deliberately doesn't ship with any shared or hardcoded data. It uses a Bring Your Own (BYO) token model: your Notion Integration Token and Database links live in Settings, are stored only in your browser's local storage, and are sent to Notion's API via a thin serverless proxy (/api/notion) that exists purely to avoid CORS restrictions.

To see new categories or accounts added directly in Notion, simply refresh the page. Adding a new transaction via the app will automatically fetch the latest data.
