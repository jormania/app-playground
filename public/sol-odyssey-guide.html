A self-run system for becoming

How to Run
Your Own Odyssey

Six weeks. One behaviour. A method you keep for life — and the app that carries it.

The idea in one breath

You don't study change. You practise it into being.

Transformation isn't an act of information or motivation. It is the residue of what you do, quietly, on ordinary days. An Odyssey is a fixed stretch of time — six weeks — in which you install one chosen behaviour by practising it daily, in the smallest honest version, witnessed by one other person.

You bring the wish to change. The method is the structure that carries it from intention to a thing you simply do. The Sol Odyssey app holds that structure for you — the charter, the daily loop, the weekly reflection, the records — so nothing rests on memory. Learn the system once; run it for the rest of your life, on anything.

01

§The Five Laws

Everything else is detail. If you forget the guide, keep these.

  • 1One behaviour at a time. An Odyssey carries a single piece of cargo — one specific, observable action, not three habits or a new life. Focus is the whole advantage; spend it on one thing and it compounds.
  • 2Tiny beats heroic. Choose a version so small it feels almost absurd — two minutes, one page, one set. A behaviour you can't fail at is one that survives bad days.
  • 3Consistency over intensity. Showing up at low power every day outperforms occasional brilliance. Forty-two unremarkable days change a person more than three perfect ones.
  • 4A system you can feel. Don't lean on motivation; it comes and goes like weather. Lean on the loop: a fixed cue, a tiny action, a daily mark, a weekly look. The app does the carrying.
  • 5Never alone. One other person who knows what you're attempting, and checks. Being witnessed changes behaviour more reliably than willpower. The buddy is the engine's second cylinder.
02

§The Engine

The whole Odyssey is one small loop, turned every day, plus a longer loop turned once a week. Learn the shape and the rest is just repetition.

  1. Do. Perform the tiny version of your behaviour — two to fifteen minutes, at your own pace. Done counts; perfect is irrelevant.
  2. Log. Mark the day and write one line — what happened, or what you noticed. Ten seconds. The mark is the reward and the memory.
  3. Reflect — weekly. Once a week, walk the five questions (§6). Read the week honestly; this is where learning happens.
  4. Adjust. Make exactly one change for the coming week — usually shrinking the behaviour or moving its anchor. Then turn the loop again.
In the app

The Today screen drives the daily loop; Weekly unlocks the reflection at each week boundary. Miss a day and you simply rejoin — the loop has no penalty, only a next turn.

03

§Before you start — your own Notion

Sol Odyssey keeps every personal record in your Notion workspace. The app ships with no account and no data of its own; it's a doorway to your databases, not a vault.

  1. Create a Notion internal integration and share your three databases with it — Odysseys, Check-ins, and Weekly Reflections.
  2. Open Settings in the app and paste your integration token and a link to each of the three databases. They're stored only in this browser, on your device — never on a server, never in the app.
  3. Tap Test Notion connection. When it confirms, you're wired end to end.
  4. Add your buddy's name and email, and (optionally) your daily check-in time and weekly call slot — recorded for you, not acted on.
  5. Optional. If you'd like a safety line (the forfeit-on-lapse commitment, below), add one text property named Commitment to your Odysseys database. Everything else works without it.
Good to know

Your token is your key to your workspace — the app holds no secret of its own. Check-ins save on your device first and sync to Notion when you're online, so a tunnel or a dead zone never loses a day. Sol Odyssey is installable as an app (a PWA) and runs offline. There's an optional guidance setting — gentle background notes along the way — that you can switch off once the method is second nature. Prefer a darker screen? A Light / Dark / System theme toggle sits top-right (this guide follows the same choice).

Optional reminders

If you like, switch on reminders in Settings: a gentle daily nudge to log (at your check-in time) and a weekly one to reflect (at your call slot), plus a heads-up when a planned Odyssey's start arrives and when you reach the summit at Day 42. Each of the four is its own switch, so keep only the ones you want. They're local and best-effort — on your device only, nothing is ever sent on your behalf, and a nudge is skipped once you've already done that day or week. Background reminders work best in the installed app on Android or desktop Chrome; on iPhone they can't run in the background, so you'll see them inside the app instead. Either way, the reminders are a scaffold, not a master — the daily message to your buddy is what truly carries the loop.

04

§The Charter

One page, written before Day 1. It turns a vague wish into a specific, daily, un-missable action. The app's charter wizard walks you through it line by line — you can begin straight away, or save it as a planned Odyssey and begin when you're ready.

How to fill each line

The wish
Say it plainly, in your own voice — one behaviour, not a whole new life. e.g. "Move my body before the day takes me."
Outcome picture
Picture the payoff vividly — what an ordinary day feels like once this is part of you. It gives the behaviour something to pull toward.
Identity — I am someone who…
Name the kind of person this makes you. Behaviour follows identity more loyally than identity follows behaviour. "…starts the day in motion."
Tiny version (the 2-minute floor)
Shrink until you cannot fail on your worst day. If unsure, halve it again. The floor must be laughably easy; you can always do more.
Anchor — after I…
Attach the action to something you already do without fail. The existing habit becomes the cue — no reminders, no willpower. "…pour my first coffee, I walk to the corner."
If-then
Name the one obstacle you can already predict, and pre-decide the response. A decision made in advance survives a tired evening.
Pairing (optional)
Optionally tie it to something you enjoy — a podcast only allowed on the walk. A small lever; leave blank if none.
Daily success
Define the lowest bar that still counts. Clear and binary: did it / didn't. No grey.
Why it matters
One honest sentence tied to a value. On the days the system isn't enough, this is what you read.
Departure
The start date — Day 1. The app defaults to the next Monday: a clean edge to begin from.
The shrink gate

Before it lets the Odyssey begin, the wizard asks: would you bet you'll do this every single day for 42 days? If you hesitate, it's still too big — go back and shrink it. The app also asks for a concrete tiny version and anchor, and requires a buddy (name + email) before you begin. Only one Odyssey can be Active at a time.

Planning ahead — the draft

You don't have to begin the moment you finish writing. Save as planned keeps the Charter as a draft you can return to and refine — and you can even prepare your next Odyssey while your current one is still running. A planned Odyssey waits in the wings: it carries no day-count and isn't your Active one yet. When you're ready, Begin now starts the 42 days (the daily loop simply counts down to Day 1 if the start date is still ahead). If a draft no longer fits, discard it — it's never begun, so nothing is lost. You can only begin a draft once any current Odyssey is harvested: one piece of cargo per voyage.

05

§The Daily Loop & Tracker

Two small acts a day, under a minute. Do the tiny version; log it. The Today screen is the whole daily ritual.

The day's check-in

  • Did the tiny version? One toggle. Done counts; perfect is irrelevant. Marking it plays a small celebration — a positive beat helps install the habit.
  • One line required — what happened, or what you noticed. Noticing your own behaviour in writing is the single highest-yield lever there is. You can't save the day without it.
  • Friction (optional) — what got in the way, if anything.
  • Sent to my buddy (optional) — a self-marked note that you reached out. The app sends nothing; the reaching out is yours.

Before Day 1, Today shows a quiet countdown. There's nothing to perform yet — just rig the system and rest.

The 42-day tracker

A 6×7 grid — six weeks of days. A filled square is a done day; an open one is a miss — no crosses, no guilt. It shows your current and best streak, but the streak isn't the point.

The one rule of the grid

Never skip two days running. One gap is an accident; two in a row is the start of a new habit pointed the wrong way. After a single gap the app surfaces a warm reminder — never a scolding. Tap any day with a note to read it back; a small check marks the days you sent to your buddy. A temperature line appears once you've logged weekly reflections.

A miss is data, not a verdict. Note the friction, rejoin tomorrow. Streaks motivate; broken streaks teach. Neither is a reason to stop.

Optional — a safety line (commitment device)

If you know you'll be tempted to drift, set yourself a safety line before Day 1: one consequence you'll honour if you ever miss two days running — a self-chosen forfeit (donate to a cause you dislike, a cold shower, a forfeited Saturday — whatever bites for you). Write it on your charter (it needs the optional Commitment property in Notion). The app then holds your word and shows it back the moment a gap opens, and again if the line is crossed — calmly, with no shame, pointing you to honour it, tell your buddy, and rejoin. Sol Odyssey witnesses; it enforces nothing — the keeping is yours. If the optional companion is on, it can help you weigh whether the line fits (enough to matter, never cruel) and meet you kindly at a lapse.

06

§The Weekly Reflection

Once a week — ideally with your buddy — you read the week as it was and change one thing. In the app, each week unlocks at its boundary; the five questions take a few minutes.

The questionThe posture
1 · The dataCount, don't judge. How many of the seven days did the tiny version happen? The app pre-fills the count from your check-ins; you confirm it.
2 · Break pointsFind the pattern. Where did it slip, and what was the real cue? Required only if a day was missed — a perfect week has nothing to diagnose.
3 · The fitSize is a dial, not a failure. Too big, too vague, or about right? Nearly every first week, it's too big.
4 · The adjustmentOne lever only. Choose exactly one change for next week — a smaller floor, a new anchor, a different time. Resist the overhaul.
5 · The temperatureTrack the shift. On 1–10, how installed does it feel — how close to automatic? Watch the drift over weeks, not any single number.

Optionally name the riskiest moment next week and a coping plan, and mark that you reflected with your buddy. Then switch seats: your buddy answers the same five, and you become the witness.

07

§The Six-Week Ascent

Each week has a different job. Don't try to do all six at once — climb one camp at a time. The summit isn't effort; it's the day the behaviour stops needing you to decide.

WeekIts job
W0 · BaseProvision. Write the charter, choose your buddy, connect Notion, set the times. Nothing to perform yet — just rig the system.
W1 · IgnitionJust arrive. Do the tiny version, mark it, tell your buddy. Don't chase more — you're proving the loop turns.
W2 · RhythmNotice. Same job, plus attention: when does it flow, where's the friction, is the anchor holding? Gather, don't change.
W3 · RecalibrateRe-size. The mid-point camp — adjust deliberately, almost always smaller or better-anchored. The most important week.
W4 · DeepenLet it grow. The floor stays tiny, but on good days allow it to expand on its own. Capacity, never obligation.
W5 · IdentityBecome. Watch the language shift from "I'm doing this" to "I'm someone who does this." That sentence is the real cargo.
W6 · SummitHarvest. Name what installed. Keep, grow, or retire it — and choose the one behaviour for the next Odyssey.
08

§The Buddy

The single highest-leverage part of the whole system, and the one most people skip. A human who knows what you're attempting and checks in turns a private resolution into a kept promise.

Choosing the right person

  • Reliable over close. A dependable acquaintance beats a beloved friend who forgets. You need someone who answers.
  • Ideally, running their own. The strongest pairing is mutual — they have their own Odyssey, you witness each other.
  • A peer, not a cheerleader. You want honest curiosity, not constant praise. Someone who'll ask "what actually happened on Thursday?"
  • One is enough. One steady witness outperforms a group chat that goes quiet.

The four ground rules

  • Witness, don't fix. Ask questions, reflect back what you hear. Advice only when asked.
  • Curiosity, never judgment. A miss is met with "what got in the way?", not disappointment.
  • Confidential. What's shared in the check-in stays there.
  • Your practice never depends on theirs. If they slip, you keep climbing.
How the app treats the buddy

Sol Odyssey records your buddy as a name and an email only, and sends nothing itself. To make reaching out effortless, each contact point — your daily check-in, weekly reflection, the kickoff, and the harvest — has a “Draft email to your buddy” button: it opens your own mail app with a tidy, pre-filled message (a recap, plus room for your own words) that you review and send. You stay in control; the app never messages anyone. If your buddy goes quiet for a few days, send one warm nudge, then carry on solo and re-sync at the weekly call. A wobble in the buddy is never a reason to stop your own loop.

An optional companion — for the in-between

The app also offers an optional AI companion: a brief, warm reflective witness you can call on the daily check-in and the weekly reflection. It mirrors back what you wrote and, at most, asks one gentle question — it never advises, coaches, or sends anything. It is not your buddy and never replaces one; think of it as something to reflect with between human check-ins, or while you're still finding your person. It's off by default: enable it in Settings with your own AI key. Your words go straight to the AI service under that key and are never saved — the reflection is just for that moment.

09

§Harvest & the next Odyssey

Day 42 isn't an ending; it's a handover. You close one Odyssey by harvesting what it grew, then point the same method at the next thing. This is how a six-week effort becomes a lifelong capability.

  1. Name what installed. What does the behaviour feel like now — automatic, half-there, still effortful? Write the honest verdict. Forty-two days rarely "finishes" a habit; it gives it roots.
  2. Keep · Grow · Retire. Decide its future — all three are wins, not grades. Keep it at the tiny floor as maintenance; grow it into a larger version next cycle; or retire it, its purpose served.
  3. Choose the next one. Pick the single behaviour for your next Odyssey. You don't start from zero — you start better at the process itself. If you lined one up as a planned Odyssey during your final week, it's already waiting — just begin it.
In the app

Open Harvest from the Charter screen (or the summit prompt on Today). Write the verdict, choose Keep / Grow / Retire — which sets the Odyssey to Maintenance, Completed, or Retired — and confirm. Harvesting leaves the active slot free, so you can begin your next Odyssey right away — including any planned Odyssey you prepared in advance.

Looking back. The Stats screen gathers who you've become across completed Odysseys — the identity you grew, what you practised, why it mattered — with no scores and no blame. From there, Save as a page exports a self-contained synopsis you can keep, print, or share.

Each Odyssey installs a behaviour and sharpens your skill at running Odysseys. By the third or fourth, the charter writes itself and the right size is obvious. The behaviours are the visible harvest; the real one is becoming a person who can deliberately change — for the rest of your life.

10

§Quick-Start — launch this week

Everything above, reduced to a departure checklist. Don't wait for the perfect Monday; the next one will do.

  1. Connect your Notion in Settings (token + three database links) and tap Test connection.
  2. Pick one behaviour to install.
  3. Walk the charter wizard — identity, tiny version, anchor, if-then, why.
  4. Shrink it until you'd bet you'll never miss — the shrink gate.
  5. Add your buddy (name + email) and agree the daily message + weekly call, off-app.
  6. Set the start date — Day 1, the coming Monday.
  7. Day 1: do the tiny version, mark it, write your line, tell your buddy. Your Odyssey has begun.

The Odyssey begins the moment you mark the first square.
