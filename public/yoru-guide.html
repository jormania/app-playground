Yoru

A wind-down for the night — one screen, one thing.

Yoru (夜, "night") does one thing at one time of day: open it in bed and let a single screen carry you down to sleep. There's no dashboard, no history, no light mode — just an optional slowing breath, a synthesised, blendable soundscape that ebbs to silence, and a place to set down whatever you're carrying into tomorrow.

Getting in

Home is bare on purpose: the pulsing 夜 glyph, a line, and Begin. Tap the glyph itself to reach Settings — that's the only way in, and it's deliberate. There's nothing else to tap.

Settings, behind the glyph

Before you begin
  • Your name — used only for the close of the night.
  • Session length — 15 to 90 minutes.
  • Soundscape — a quick-pick starting blend, refined in the mixer below.
  • Audio tuner — opens the full mixer.
  • Display mode — stay lit, go dark, or turn off (see below).
  • Atmosphere — Night, Moonlight, or Candlelight — a palette, not a setting that changes behaviour.
  • Breathwork + pattern — only shown while the screen stays lit.

Every change here is auditioned live through a single sound preview, so you hear a change before you commit to a whole night of it.

The soundscape mixer

Fully synthesised — no audio files. Rather than picking one fixed scene, Yoru blends independent layers, the way the better ambient-noise apps do:

  • Rain — a wash plus stereo droplets
  • Waves — slow surf
  • Wind — drifting, occasionally gusting
  • Leaves — wind through foliage, with rustles
  • Warmth — a pink-noise floor, warmer and less boomy than brown noise
  • Drone — a soft low fifth underneath everything

Four shapers sit alongside the layers: Volume (master), Brightness (one global low-pass, dark to airy), Motion (how much the layers swell and gust), and Pace (how fast they drift). Nature only — no animals, no city.

Whatever the mix, the sound never simply cuts: it fades in gently, ebbs to true silence by the session's end on its own, and releases softly if you stop early.

Breathwork (optional)

A breathing orb offers two patterns: a lengthening exhale (the default — the exhale grows across the session) or a fixed 4·7·8. Turn it off for sound alone; the centre then holds a still 夜. Tap the orb — or the glyph, sound-only — to end the night early. A quiet mm:ss countdown sits in the bottom-right, easy to miss on purpose.

Display mode: lit · dark · off

Stay lit

The orb stays on screen, dimming gradually toward the end of the session.

Go dark

Blacks out to a live night sky (below) while staying awake, so the soundscape keeps playing. Tap anywhere to peek back at the time remaining.

Turn off

Releases the wake lock so the device's screen can genuinely sleep and save power. Re-engaging flips it back to lit.

You set the starting mode in Settings, but you can change it any time during the night from a quiet lit · dark · off control at the top of the screen — always there when the screen is lit, and revealed with a tap-to-peek when it's dark or off. Switching never interrupts the night: the sound, the timer and the breath carry on; only the screen changes. So you might follow the breath lit, go dark to rest with the sky, then turn off to drift away on sound alone.

The night sky

What you see in "go dark":

  • Generic drifting stars — no constellations or zodiac.
  • The real moon, at tonight's true phase, placed using your device's location where you've granted it (otherwise a gentle default) — rendered as a lit body with maria and limb darkening, not a flat plate.
  • Mount Fuji, a static, dim, moonlit silhouette on the horizon, unchanged across seasons.
  • Season-aware drift: sakura in spring, fireflies in summer, momiji in autumn, snow in winter.

The note

A place to set a single thought down — not a journal, and it isn't kept. Write it, tap "set it down," and it's out of view for the rest of the night. At the session's end it's discarded for good; the point is offloading it before sleep, not keeping a record.

Everything else

  • Resumes within the same night if you close the app partway through; starts fresh the next night.
  • Works fully offline and installs as a PWA.
  • Nothing leaves your device — no account, no server, no sync.
