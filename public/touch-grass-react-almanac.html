// almanac of moments

The turning year, marked on the card.

On these days the sky and the finds turn special. The home-screen tagline and the discovery you bring back are coloured by the moment; some of them change the sky itself. Here is every one, so you can anticipate them — roughly thirty-four across the year. Dates are approximate (±1 day) and a few shift with the moon.

// solar turning points

The four hinges of the solar year. Finds lean into the balance or extremity of light.

Mar 20
The Spring Equinox
Day and night in perfect balance as the year tilts toward growth. Finds turn to renewal and first green.
Jun 21
The Summer Solstice
The longest day, the sun crowned at its zenith. Finds of light at its most generous.
Sep 22
The Autumn Equinox
The scales of light and dark level again before the long descent. Finds tip toward the dark.
Dec 21
The Winter Solstice
The longest night, the still point where the light is reborn. Finds of stillness and quiet return.

// the cross-quarter sabbats

The old fire-festivals between the solstices and equinoxes. Their mood seeps into the find.

Feb 1
Imbolc
The first quickening beneath frozen ground — a single candle against winter.
May 1
Beltane
The fire-festival of bloom and union, spring tipping into summer.
Aug 1
Lughnasadh
The first harvest — the year beginning, quietly, to die.
Oct 31
Samhain
The night the veil wears thin and the dead draw near. The most haunted finds of the year (Oct 31–Nov 1).

// meteor showers

These change the sky: on clear nights, real shooting stars streak across the card.

Each peaks for a night or two (±1 day). Finds are touched by falling fire.

Jan 3
The Quadrantids
A brief, sharp fall of winter meteors.
Apr 22
The Lyrids
Old comet-dust streaking the spring sky.
May 6
The Eta Aquariids
Fragments of Halley raining before dawn.
Jul 30
The Delta Aquariids
A slow drizzle of midsummer meteors.
Aug 12
The Perseids
The sky shedding fire on the warmest nights — the year's best-loved shower.
Oct 8
The Draconids
The dragon breathing sparks low in the north.
Oct 21
The Orionids
Swift meteors loosed from the hunter.
Nov 17
The Leonids
The lion's brief storm of shooting stars.
Dec 14
The Geminids
The richest fall of the year, slow and bright.
Dec 22
The Ursids
A quiet shower around the longest night.

// the moon

These change the sky: the moon shows its true phase every night, and these two are marked.

monthly
A Full Moon
The moon full and watchful, the tides of the strange at their height. The bright, bare faced disc.
monthly
A New Moon
The moon gone dark, the sky holding its breath. The disc wears its deepest shadow.

// earth's orbit

Jan 4
Perihelion
Earth swung closest to the sun, though the world lies cold.
Jul 4
Aphelion
Earth at its farthest from the sun, though the world lies warm.
